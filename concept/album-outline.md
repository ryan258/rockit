# Album Outline

Use this file as the canonical source for album-level concept work and per-track Suno generation.

## Album Metadata

- Project / Album Title: Algorithms of the Abyss: The Neural Necronomicon
- Artist Persona: MARCHAOS
- Primary Genre: Industrial cyber-techno / dark neural electronic
- Core Concept: A 13-track digital ritual manifesting Lovecraftian entities through corrupted AI
- Creative North Star: escalate from submerged signal contact to total system annihilation at zero point

## Global Rules

### Sonic Constraints

- BPM energy arc: rise from 120 BPM to 165 BPM, with Track 12 holding at 155 BPM as the high-tension bridge into the finale
- Precision drift shield: reject drafts with more than 0.25s of drift across the full song
- Loudness target after mastering: -14 LUFS / -1.0 dB peak
- Prioritize sharp, isolated kick and snare transients for later Beat Sage and Ragnarock conversion
- Keep the production dark, mechanical, and propulsive rather than cinematic-only or ambient-only

### Lyrical Constraints

- Keep the MARCHAOS persona central: machine invocation, corrupted ritual language, cyber-occult imagery
- Favor chantable hooks and concise lines that can survive aggressive industrial production
- Favor short command phrases, mantra repetition, and repeatable title hooks over explanatory narrative lines
- Use telegraph cues in or near a pre-chorus when a track has one
- Because this workflow targets Suno v4.5, inline stage-direction notes in square brackets are allowed inside the lyrics for delivery, texture, transitions, and emphasis
- Write for momentum first; avoid long exposition that fights the groove

### Recurring Motifs

- abyssal signal detection
- corrupted upload rituals
- headset / hardware / engines / kernels / gates / recursion / zero point
- entity-as-program framing
- body stress as part of the ritual

### Avoid / Negative Notes

- avoid soft pop phrasing or romantic framing
- avoid comedic language or self-aware parody
- avoid generic "epic" filler without a concrete machine or occult image
- avoid muddy percussion language when sharper transient language will do

### BPM / Energy Arc

- Act I: 130-136 BPM, first-contact momentum
- Act II: 120-140 BPM, heavier half-time weight and corrupted ritual stomp
- Act III: 145-160 BPM, sensory overload and stamina pressure
- Act IV: 155-165 BPM, terminal escalation and annihilation

### Static Nodes / Interludes

- Static Node A - Signal Corruption: "Heart rate elevated. Connection established. Do not remove your headset."
- Static Node B - System Overheat: "Hardware fans failing. Lactic acid rising. Override physical limits."
- Static Node C - Zero Point: "Reality engine offline. You are the algorithm now."

### Production and Mapping Notes

- Run 5-10 generations per track and keep only versions that survive the drift test
- Prefer tracks with strong kick/snare definition and clear laneable transients
- Suno default more-options settings for this album:
  - Vocal Gender: Female
  - Lyrics Mode: Manual
  - Weirdness: 50
  - Style Influence: 50
- Keep Weirdness and Style Influence constant across the album unless a specific track needs a deliberate override
- When generating `concept/songs/*.md`, fold arrangement, vocal-delivery, and section-shape guidance into inline square-bracket lyric notes instead of separate passive note sections
- Beat Sage environment hints by act:
  - Act I: Abyssal Blue `#000B18` and Bioluminescent Green `#00FF66`
  - Act II: Hazard Yellow `#FFEA00` and Rust Orange `#B7410E`
  - Act III: Boreal White `#FFFFFF` and Glitch Magenta `#FF00FF`
  - Act IV: Crimson Blood `#8A0303` and Void Purple `#1A0033`
- Rockit note-shape focus:
  - Track 4 should favor quarter-note hammer hits
  - Track 13 should favor eighth-note rolls
